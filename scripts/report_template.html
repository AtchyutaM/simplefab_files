2. Demand Model

Demand is deterministic and pre-computed at configuration time. Total demand derives from the target utilization of the bottleneck machines (M0/M2).

Result: 272 units of P0 + 272 units of P1 = 544 total demand, distributed as 68 units per product per week arriving at ticks 96, 768, 1440, and 2112.

Raw material arrivals arrive at 100% capacity (296 units per product), ensuring the factory always has more supply than demand. The challenge is purely about scheduling and sequencing, not supply constraints.

Demand Fulfillment Logic

When Machine 3 finishes a unit:

  • If demand exists for that product → ship immediately, earn revenue
  • If no demand → store as finished inventory (holding cost accumulates each tick)

Unfulfilled demand accumulates as backorders, incurring per-tick penalties ($0.10/P0, $0.15/P1) until fulfilled.

3. RL Training Method

Algorithm: MaskablePPO

We use Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) with invalid action masking from the sb3-contrib library. Action masking ensures the agent only selects feasible actions at each timestep — it cannot start a busy machine, nor batch a product with fewer than 4 units in queue.

Reward Signal

The per-step reward has three components:

  1. ΔProfit — change in cumulative profit (Revenue − all costs)
  2. Invalid Action Penalty — −$2.0 for any masked action the agent selects
  3. Potential-Based Shaping — β(γΦ(s') − Φ(s))

Where Φ(s) = −(2.0 × backlog + 0.2 × WIP + 0.05 × finished_inv). This provides a denser learning signal, guiding the agent to reduce backlogs proactively rather than waiting for the delayed revenue.

Why These 8 New Dimensions Matter

  • Demand Lookahead (20–23): The agent can see "68 units of P0 are coming this week" before they enter the backlog. This enables proactive, just-in-time production that reduces backorder spikes.
  • Time Awareness (24–25): Period progress tells the agent "demand is about to arrive" within the weekly cycle. Global progress signals "this is the last week — don't overproduce."
  • Setup Context (26–27): When M1 or M3 are idle, the agent knows what they last processed. This enables informed decisions about whether a product switch (and the $20 cost) is worthwhile.

5. Problem Constraints Summary

The scheduling problem is deeply constrained. The factory operates under extreme load:

  • Time Horizon: 4 Weeks (2688 ticks = 1 tick per 15 minutes)
  • Arrivals: Raw materials arrive weekly covering exactly 100% of the bottleneck's capacity.
  • Demand: Customers place orders weekly at 92% of capacity.
  • Slack: Only 240 ticks of slack (2.5 days out of 28 days) are left across M1 and M3. A single unnecessary changeover burns 1 tick.

6. RL Agent Performance vs Baseline Heuristic

What is the Baseline Heuristic (Commander)?

The "Commander" is a hand-coded, greedy pull-system algorithm. It always prioritizes whichever product currently has the highest percentage share of the unmet customer demand backorder queue. It does not look ahead, and it does not natively understand that switching products incurs a setup time penalty.
